The Story of Diwali

Diwali, known as the "Festival of Lights," is one of the most important festivals in Hinduism. It celebrates the profound victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance.

The most famous story behind Diwali is the return of Lord Rama to his kingdom of Ayodhya after 14 long years of exile. Along with his wife Sita and his loyal brother Lakshmana, he was forced to leave his kingdom and live in the forest.

During this exile, the demon king Ravana of Lanka abducted Sita. Lord Rama, with the help of the mighty monkey god Hanuman and his army, fought a great battle against Ravana and defeated him, rescuing Sita and restoring righteousness.

To celebrate the triumphant return of their beloved king, the people of Ayodhya lit rows of clay lamps (diyas) to illuminate the path and welcome them home. This beautiful tradition continues today, with people lighting diyas and setting off fireworks to signify the timeless triumph of good and light.
